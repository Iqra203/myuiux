E-commerce Mobile App UI

A case study on designing a minimalist and user-friendly mobile shopping experience.

ROLE UI/UX Designer, Prototyping
TOOLS Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop
TIMELINE 4 Weeks

The Problem

Many e-commerce apps are cluttered and difficult to navigate, leading to user frustration and high cart abandonment rates. The primary challenge was to design an app that simplifies the shopping process, from product discovery to checkout, making it feel effortless and enjoyable for the user.

The Goal

The main goal was to create an intuitive and visually appealing mobile app that would increase user engagement and boost conversion rates. Key objectives included: creating a seamless browsing experience, simplifying the checkout process, and establishing a clean, modern brand identity.

My Design Process

My process began with competitive analysis and user research to understand pain points in existing apps. I then moved to creating user personas and journey maps. Based on this, I developed low-fidelity wireframes, followed by high-fidelity mockups and an interactive prototype in Figma. Usability testing was conducted at the end to validate the design choices.

Wireframes & Prototypes

Early wireframes focused on core functionality and user flow, ensuring a logical structure before adding visual details.

The Solution & Final Designs

The final design features a clean layout, a minimalist color palette, and intuitive navigation. A one-page checkout process was implemented to reduce friction. Product pages were designed to highlight high-quality imagery and provide essential information clearly, creating a premium and trustworthy user experience.

What I Learned

This project reinforced the importance of user-centric design. Conducting user testing early in the process was invaluable, as it helped identify usability issues before development. I also learned a lot about creating a scalable design system in Figma that ensures consistency across all screens.
